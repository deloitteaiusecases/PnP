## vi	Lubrication Management

Introduction and Background of the Section
Lubrication is a foundational pillar of equipment reliability at Arabian Mills. As part of a structured preventive maintenance program, lubrication activities are fully embedded within SAP PM workflows and are executed in accordance with defined schedules, technical standards, and equipment-specific requirements.
All lubrication tasks are guided by OEM recommendations, engineering judgment, and historical failure data. They are implemented through standard SAP task lists and checklists to ensure consistent execution, proper documentation, and continuous tracking of lubricant consumption and effectiveness.
The system ensures that the right lubricant is applied, in the right amount, to the right point, at the right interval—resulting in reduced wear, fewer breakdowns, extended asset life, and measurable operational efficiency.
Procedure for Lubrication

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Review and Maintain Lubrication Standards**<br>• Ensure lubrication points, frequency, and lubricant specifications are aligned with OEM and internal best practices. | Maintenance Supervisor | Annual Review |
| 2 | **Assign SAP Task Lists**<br>• Link pre-approved lubrication task lists and checklists to preventive maintenance schedules in SAP PM. | Maintenance Planner | One-time / Updates |
| 3 | **Generate Lubrication Work Orders**<br>• Work orders are automatically generated from SAP PM schedules and assigned to certified technicians. | Maintenance Planner | Monthly |
| 4 | **Perform Lubrication Activities**<br>• Technicians carry out lubrication tasks using standard methods and safety protocols, referring to SAP checklists. | Technician | As per Schedule |
| 5 | **Record Execution Details in SAP**<br>• Technicians document completed tasks, lubricant quantities used, and any abnormalities observed. | Technician | After Each Task |
| 6 | **Supervisor Validation**<br>• Supervisors verify task completion, quality of feedback, and raise work notifications if defects are found. | Maintenance Supervisor | Daily |
| 7 | **Inventory and Consumption Monitoring**<br>• Lubricant usage and stock levels are tracked digitally, with automated alerts for reorder thresholds. | Storekeeper | Weekly |
| 8 | **Performance Monitoring**<br>• Lubrication effectiveness is reviewed using SAP-generated KPIs and failure trend analysis. | Maintenance Manager | Monthly |

Roles and Responsibilities – Lubrication Management

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Perform scheduled lubrication using SAP task lists and checklist guidance.<br>• - Log quantities used and observations in SAP PM. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Maintain and update lubrication standards based on performance trends and equipment upgrades.<br>• - Review technician feedback and validate quality and completeness of work.<br>• - Initiate corrective notifications as needed. |
| Maintenance Planner | • - Generate lubrication work orders through SAP and assign to qualified personnel.<br>• - Ensure integration of updated task lists. |
| Storekeeper | - Maintain lubricant inventory, issue stock, and flag anomalies in usage or reordering. |
| SAP PM Administrator | - Ensure task lists are properly linked, updated, and functioning in SAP PM. - Support compliance tracking and reporting. |
| Maintenance Manager | • - Oversee adherence to lubrication policy and ensure strategic alignment with reliability goals.<br>• - Review lubrication-related failures periodically<br>• - Monitor lubrication KPIs and lead improvement initiatives. |
