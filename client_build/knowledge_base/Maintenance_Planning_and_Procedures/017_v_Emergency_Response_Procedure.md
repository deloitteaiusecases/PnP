## v	Emergency Response Procedure

Introduction and Background of the Section
Emergency maintenance situations—such as mechanical failures, equipment fires, critical safety system breakdowns, or power system malfunctions—pose serious risks to production continuity, personnel safety, and asset integrity. These events demand immediate, well-coordinated, and technically sound responses.
In a complex plant environment like Arabian Mills, the maintenance team must be prepared to respond rapidly, contain risk, restore function, and maintain traceability through post-event documentation and analysis. This procedure ensures that emergency interventions are not reactive in isolation but are strategically embedded in the maintenance control system and tied to continuous improvement.
Procedure

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Detect and Report Emergency**<br>• Any equipment failure or critical abnormality is immediately reported via radio, phone, or plant alert systems. | Technician / Operator | As Occurs |
| 2 | **Initial Risk Assessment and Communication**<br>• Maintenance Supervisor evaluates the situation for safety and operational impact, then alerts concerned departments. | Maintenance Supervisor | Immediate |
| 3 | **Authorize Emergency Work**<br>• Maintenance Supervisor authorizes the technician to proceed, subject to verbal safety clearance and field risk checks. | Maintenance Supervisor | Immediate |
| 4 | **Initiate Temporary Controls**<br>• Implement temporary isolation, tagging, or containment actions to stabilize the situation. | Maintenance Supervisor | Immediate |
| 5 | **Execute Repair**<br>• Carry out the necessary repair or workaround under supervisor oversight. Ensure safety protocols (PPE, THA) are followed. | Technician | As Needed |
| 6 | **Create Emergency Work Order in SAP**<br>• After stabilizing the situation, create a formal SAP work order for documentation and closure tracking. | Maintenance Supervisor | Within 24 Hours |
| 7 | **Record Confirmation and Feedback**<br>• Technician enters detailed feedback on actions taken, materials used, time, and observations. | Technician | Post Task |
| 8 | **Supervisor Review and Closure**<br>• Supervisor verifies completion, accuracy of feedback, and closes the emergency work order in SAP. | Maintenance Supervisor | Same Day |
| 9 | **Post-Incident Review and Root Cause Analysis**<br>• Significant failures are reviewed for root causes and potential preventive actions. | Maintenance Manager | Within 7 Days |
| 10 | **Update Maintenance Strategy if Required**<br>• Adjust PM plans, inspection frequencies, or training needs based on learning from the incident. | Maintenance Manager | As Needed |

Roles and Responsibilities – Emergency Maintenance

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Immediately report emergencies via radio or phone.<br>• - Execute emergency repairs using proper PPE and safety protocols.<br>• - Record time, materials, and feedback in SAP. |
| Operator | - Detect and escalate critical equipment failures or abnormalities to the maintenance team. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Assess the emergency, authorize work, and ensure safety controls are in place.<br>• - Oversee execution and validate SAP entries.<br>• - Approve and close emergency work orders. |
| Maintenance Planner | • - Create emergency work orders in SAP if not already generated.<br>• - Track completion and documentation status. |
| Maintenance Manager | • - Lead root cause analysis (RCA) for significant breakdowns.<br>• - Recommend updates to PM plans or inspection frequencies based on findings.<br>• - Monitor emergency event trends and systemic issues.<br>• - Ensure preventive actions from RCA are implemented across the department. |
| SAP PM Administrator | • - Ensure proper classification of emergency work orders in SAP.<br>• - Maintain accurate reporting and support audit readiness. |
