## .2. Open Data Principles

Open data is one of the NDMO domains for data management.  should adhere to the following principles:
1. Open by Default:  should be ensured that the data should be available for all through disclosing, providing access to, or using such data, unless its nature requires non-disclosure or protection of its privacy or confidentiality.
2. Open Format and Machine-Readable: Data shall be stored in different file formats (such as CSV, XLS, JSON, XML) and made publicly accessible in a machine-readable format that allows automated processing.
3. Up To Date:  shall be regularly published the latest versions of open datasets and made available to the public in a timely basis. Whenever feasible, data collected by  shall be released as quickly as it is gathered. Priority shall be given to data whose utility is time sensitive.
4. Comprehensive: Open datasets shall be as complete and as granular as possible, reflecting recorded data, in compliance with the Personal Data Privacy Policy. Metadata that defines and explains raw data shall be included along with explanations or formulas for how data was extracted or calculated.
5. Non-discriminatory: Open datasets shall be made available for all without discrimination or requirement for registration. Any person shall be able to access the published open data at any time without having to identify him or to provide justification for gaining access.
6. Free of Charge: Open data shall be made available for all free of charge.
7. Improved Governance and Citizen Engagement: Open data shall enable data access and engagement for all, reinforce the transparency and accountability of , and support the process of decision-making and provision of public services
8. Inclusive Development and Innovation:  shall play an active role in promoting the reuse of open data and providing the necessary supporting resources and expertise. These entities shall, in conjunction with the relevant stakeholders, seek to empower a future generation of open data innovators and engaging individuals, organizations, and the general public in unlocking the value of open data.
9. Open Data Platform: Any datasets in the Data Catalogue that can be made publicly available shall be listed on the  Open Data Platform. The Data Catalogue shall include datasets that can be made publicly available but have not yet been released.
10. Language: All published data descriptions shall be provided in plain language.
11. Quality of data: All published open datasets shall include an indication on the age of the data and an indication on the quality expected from the data.
