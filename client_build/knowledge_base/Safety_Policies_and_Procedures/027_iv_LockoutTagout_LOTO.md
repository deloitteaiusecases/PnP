## iv	Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

Introduction and Background of the Section
Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) is a globally recognized safety practice used to control hazardous energy during the servicing and maintenance of machinery. Its purpose is to prevent the accidental startup or release of stored energy that could cause injury to personnel or damage to equipment.
At present, Arabian Mills relies on informal electrical isolation performed at the electrical panel. However, there is no structured LOTO program, no standardized procedures, and no tagging or verification steps. This exposes technicians to significant safety risks during mechanical and electrical maintenance.
Adopting a formal LOTO program is not only a compliance measure but a critical step toward building a safety-first maintenance culture. It will cover electrical, mechanical, pneumatic, hydraulic, thermal, and chemical energy sources, and apply to all departments and contractors performing maintenance activities.
Policy
Arabian Mills shall implement a formal Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) program to protect personnel from unexpected equipment startup or hazardous energy release during maintenance and servicing.
The LOTO system shall be:
 Mandatory for all maintenance activities where there is risk of stored or residual energy
 Aligned with international safety standards, including OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147
 Applied consistently to electrical, mechanical, pneumatic, and hydraulic systems
 Standardized through written procedures, lockout devices, padlocks, and tags
 Supervised by authorized personnel with clearly defined roles and responsibilities
 Audited regularly for compliance, effectiveness, and continuous improvement
Failure to follow LOTO procedures shall be treated as a serious safety violation, subject to disciplinary action.
Procedure for Development and Implementation of a LOTO System

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Identify Equipment Requiring LOTO**<br>• Survey all machinery to determine where hazardous energy isolation is necessary. | Maintenance Manager / Safety Officer | One-time / Annual Review |
| 2 | **Develop Equipment-Specific LOTO Procedures**<br>• Prepare written step-by-step instructions for each equipment type, including all energy isolation points. | Maintenance Manager / Safety Officer | One-time / Updates |
| 3 | **Procure LOTO Devices and Accessories**<br>• Acquire padlocks, lockout hasps, tags, and isolation kits for electrical, pneumatic, and hydraulic systems. | Safety Officer | One-time / As Needed |
| 4 | **Install Lockout Hardware and Identification Labels**<br>• Label energy isolation points on all applicable equipment. | Maintenance Team | One-time |
| 5 | **Train Personnel**<br>• Conduct formal training for technicians, supervisors, and contractors on LOTO procedures and responsibilities. | Safety Officer | Initial + Annual |
| 6 | **Implement LOTO for Maintenance Jobs**<br>• Require LOTO application before any task involving exposure to energy hazards. | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 7 | **Verify Zero Energy State**<br>• Confirm isolation by testing before maintenance begins. | Technician | Per Task |
| 8 | **Document Lockout/Tagout in Work Order**<br>• Attach LOTO record in SAP as part of work order confirmation. | SAP PM Administrator | Per Task |
| 9 | **Audit LOTO Implementation**<br>• Conduct routine inspections of LOTO application for compliance. | Safety Officer | Monthly |

Roles and Responsibilities – Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Apply lockout devices and tags before performing maintenance.<br>• - Test equipment to confirm zero energy state.<br>• - Record LOTO usage in SAP work order. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Authorize the maintenance job and ensure that LOTO has been applied correctly.<br>• - Monitor compliance during task execution. |
| Maintenance Manager | • - Identify energy sources and develop detailed equipment-specific LOTO procedures.<br>• - Ensure procedures are updated with equipment changes. |
| Safety Officer | • - Develop LOTO program, conduct training, and lead audits.<br>• - Monitor compliance and report non-conformities. |
| Maintenance Director | • - Ensure LOTO policy enforcement and resource allocation.<br>• - Review audit results and approve corrective actions. |
| Storekeeper | - Maintain stock of LOTO devices and ensure timely replenishment. |
| SAP PM Administrator | - Ensure LOTO documentation is recorded properly in SAP work orders and reports. |
