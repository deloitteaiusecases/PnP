# Safety Policies and Procedures

Introduction and background of the Section
Maintenance work inherently involves elevated risk compared to normal operations. Technicians may work around energized equipment, confined spaces, elevated platforms, rotating machinery, combustible dust, and pressurized systems. These conditions require strict adherence to safety protocols to prevent injury, equipment damage, and regulatory violations.
While the Safety Department is responsible for policy development, training, audits, and compliance monitoring, the Maintenance Department must take ownership of implementing safe work practices, risk identification, and reporting within its day-to-day activities.
Arabian Mills adopts a risk-based approach to maintenance safety aligned with international standards including:
 ISO 45001 – Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
 ILO Guidelines on Occupational Safety and Health Management Systems
 NFPA 70E / OSHA 1910 Subpart S – Electrical safety in maintenance
 SMRP Best Practices – Maintenance safety integration
Integration of safety into maintenance processes—particularly through Task Hazard Analysis (THA), Work Permits, and a robust Incident Reporting System—is essential for fostering a preventive safety culture.
Policy
 All maintenance tasks—whether routine or non-routine—must be assessed for safety risks using a documented Task Hazard Analysis (THA) prior to execution. First-time tasks or jobs with elevated risk must undergo enhanced scrutiny. PPE usage and relevant Work Permits (e.g., for confined space, hot work, working at height, or energy isolation) must be followed as per established safety protocols.
 All maintenance personnel are responsible for reporting unsafe conditions, behaviours, or incidents through the SAP Incident Reporting System. A strong safety culture shall be promoted through daily leadership behaviours, structured toolbox talks, cross-functional safety participation, and visible commitment to safe work practices.
