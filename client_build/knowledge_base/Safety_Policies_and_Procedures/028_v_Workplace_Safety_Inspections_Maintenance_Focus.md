## v	Workplace Safety Inspections (Maintenance Focus)

Introduction and Background of the Section
Maintenance activities inherently involve high-risk tasks such as working on energized equipment, operating in confined spaces, handling heavy machinery, or performing jobs at height. These risks can increase significantly in environments like a wheat milling or animal feed plant due to combustible dust, rotating components, and thermal processes.
A formal Workplace Safety Inspection system focused on maintenance activities is essential to:
 Identify unsafe conditions or behaviors before incidents occur
 Reinforce compliance with PPE, LOTO, THA, and work permit systems
 Ensure that tools, equipment, and safety controls are in good condition
 Promote a culture of accountability and proactive hazard control
This section establishes the framework for developing, conducting, and acting upon routine safety inspections specifically targeted at maintenance workspaces and practices.
Procedure for Development and Implementation of a Workplace Safety Inspection System

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Define Inspection Scope and Criteria**<br>• Identify key safety elements to be checked (e.g., PPE compliance, LOTO, tools, housekeeping, fire hazards). | Safety Officer / Maintenance Manager | One-time / Annual Review |
| 2 | **Develop Standard Inspection Checklist**<br>• Create a checklist covering high-risk maintenance areas and practices. Include reference to applicable standards (e.g., LOTO, THA, PPE). | Safety Officer | One-time / Updates |
| 3 | **Assign Inspection Responsibilities**<br>• Designate safety champions or supervisors to conduct field inspections with trained oversight. | Safety Officer | Monthly Assignment |
| 4 | **Conduct Workplace Inspections**<br>• Inspect job sites, workshops, and ongoing maintenance activities. Document observations and take immediate action on unsafe conditions. | Supervisor / Safety Officer | Weekly / Random |
| 5 | **Record and Report Findings**<br>• Submit completed inspection checklists to the Safety Officer. | Inspector | Per Inspection |
| 6 | **Assign and Track Corrective Actions**<br>• Assign open safety items to responsible personnel for closure with due dates. | Safety Officer / Maintenance Supervisor | Ongoing |
| 7 | **Review and Analyze Trends**<br>• Analyze findings to identify recurring risks and recommend improvements in procedures or training. | Safety Officer | Monthly |
| 8 | **Communicate Results**<br>• Share inspection summaries in toolbox talks and departmental safety reviews. | Maintenance Manager | Monthly |

Roles and Responsibilities – Workplace Safety Inspections

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Cooperate during inspections and comply with corrective actions.<br>• - Report any unsafe conditions observed in the work area. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Conduct regular safety inspections.<br>• - Ensure immediate correction of unsafe behaviors or setups.<br>• - Report and track actions. |
| Maintenance Manager | • - Support inspection scope design and checklist development.<br>• - Follow up on engineering-related safety improvements. |
| Safety Officer | • - Lead the inspection program.<br>• - Provide training, tools, and enforcement.<br>• - Maintain inspection records and perform trend analysis. |
| Maintenance Director | • - Ensure enforcement of inspection policy.<br>• - Review monthly reports and close systemic gaps.<br>• - Recognize positive safety practices. |
