## Maintenance Related Risks

Introduction and Background of the Section
Maintenance-related risks are dynamic and task-specific, ranging from mechanical hazards (e.g., pinch points, rotating equipment) to environmental exposures (e.g., dust, noise, confined spaces). These risks are further amplified when work is unplanned, performed under pressure, or executed during running operations.
The Maintenance Department plays a frontline role in identifying and controlling risks before work begins, using structured Task Hazard Analysis (THA) and ensuring continuous awareness through SAP-linked documentation and incident tracking. This section defines the structured process for risk recognition, control, and reporting as embedded in the maintenance workflow.
In addition to supporting policies and conducting audits, the Safety Officer plays a vital role in validating high-risk maintenance activities. For jobs involving confined spaces, energized systems, working at height, or first-time execution, the Safety Officer must review the Task Hazard Analysis (THA), verify that required control measures and permits are in place, and confirm that the job can proceed safely. This ensures alignment with corporate HSE standards and regulatory compliance.
Procedure for Maintenance Related Risks

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Conduct Task Hazard Analysis (THA)**<br>• Before executing any non-routine, high-risk, or first-time job, complete a THA identifying potential hazards and mitigation measures. | Technician | Per Task |
| 2 | **Review and Approve THA**<br>• Supervisor verifies THA content for thoroughness, checks mitigation measures, and signs off before job start. | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 3 | **Safety Officer THA Validation**<br>• For high-risk or first-time jobs, the Safety Officer must review the THA, confirm adequacy of controls and PPE, and validate job readiness. | Safety Officer | As Needed |
| 4 | **First-Time Task Protocol**<br>• For tasks never performed before, require joint pre-job walkthrough by technician, supervisor, engineer, and Safety Officer. Maintenance Manager sign-off is mandatory. | Technician / Supervisor / Safety Officer | As Needed |
| 5 | **Integrate THA into PM/CM Workflow**<br>• For recurring tasks, key risks and controls identified in past THAs must be embedded into PM task lists or standard CM procedures in SAP. | Maintenance Planner | Quarterly |
| 6 | **Execute with Controls**<br>• Ensure that all safety controls defined in the THA are implemented before and during job execution, including LOTO, PPE, tools, and access control. | Technician | Per Task |
| 7 | **Toolbox Talks and Briefings**<br>• Conduct daily toolbox talks highlighting planned jobs, THA summaries, PPE requirements, and lessons from past incidents. | Maintenance Supervisor | Daily |
| 8 | **Use of PPE and Work Permits**<br>• Confirm that correct PPE is issued and worn. Secure appropriate work permits before starting jobs that require isolation, hot work, confined entry, or elevation. | Supervisor / Safety Officer | Per Task |
| 9 | **Report Safety Incidents in SAP**<br>• Any near miss, injury, or unsafe act/condition must be logged in SAP within 24 hours using the incident reporting module. | Technician / Supervisor | As Occurs |
| 10 | **Analyse Recurring Risks**<br>• Review THA patterns and incident data to identify systemic risks and recommend engineering or procedural interventions. | Maintenance Manager | Quarterly |
| 11 | **Promote Safety Culture in Maintenance**<br>• Lead by example, recognize safe behaviours , enforce accountability, and ensure full integration of safety into planning and execution routines. | Maintenance Director | Ongoing |

Roles and Responsibilities – Maintenance-Related Safety Risks

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | Complete THA, implement safety controls, use PPE, report incidents, and stop work if unsafe |
| Maintenance Supervisor | Review and approve THAs, conduct toolbox talks, ensure permit compliance, and lead incident response |
| Maintenance Manager | Support first-time task assessment, analyze recurring risks, and recommend safety design improvements |
| Maintenance Planner | Integrate THA learnings into PM/CM task lists in SAP and ensure alignment with risk mitigation standards |
| SAP PM Administrator | Link THAs and incident reports to work orders in SAP and support safety data reporting |
| Maintenance Director | Champion safety culture, approve first-time task reviews, review incident trends, and drive cross-functional improvement |
| Safety Officer | Validate THAs for high-risk/first-time jobs, ensure proper control measures, authorize job start for permit-required work, and support compliance reviews |

How a THA Is Performed
1. Technician identifies the task to be done and completes the THA form covering:
  o Description of the job steps
  o Hazards associated with each step
  o Controls or precautions required (LOTO, PPE, barriers, supervision)
2. Supervisor reviews the form, confirms the mitigation measures are adequate, checks for required permits or PPE, and signs off before job starts.
3. For first-time tasks, a pre-job review is conducted with a maintenance engineer, and the Maintenance Manager’s sign-off is required. The THA is retained for future updates to PM/CM checklists in SAP.
4. All THAs are attached to work orders in SAP for traceability, and high-risk jobs are escalated if necessary.
Example of a THA Template

| Section | Information Required | Inputs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Task Description | Describe the task to be performed |  |
| 2. Location of Work | Exact area or system where the job will occur |  |
| 3. Date and Time | Planned date and time of the activity |  |
| 4. Supervisor in Charge | Name of the responsible supervisor |  |
| 5. Maintenance Technician(s) | Names of technicians involved |  |
| 6. Equipment or Asset ID | SAP equipment number or tag |  |
| 7. Permit(s) Required (Yes/No, Type) | E.g., LOTO, Hot Work, Confined Space, Working at Height |  |
| 8. PPE Required | List required PPE (e.g., gloves, goggles, helmet, respirator) |  |
| 9. Step-by-Step Job Breakdown | Break the task into sequential steps |  |
| 10. Associated Hazards | List possible hazards for each step (e.g., pinch points, sparks, inhalation) |  |
| 11. Risk Control Measures | Describe control actions (e.g., barriers, LOTO, ventilation) |  |
| 12. Tools and Equipment to be Used | Specify tools/machines needed (e.g., grinder, ladder, tester) |  |
| 13. Safety Officer Review (Yes/No) | For high-risk jobs or first-time tasks, Safety Officer must review |  |
| 14. Approval Signatures | Sign-off by Technician, Supervisor, and Safety Officer (if applicable) |  |

Safety Culture Development in the Maintenance Department
To embed a safety-first culture in the Maintenance Department:
 Supervisors will reinforce safety in daily briefings and job assignments
 Leadership will recognize and reward proactive safety behavior
 Maintenance personnel will be trained in THA, LOTO, and SAP-based safety reporting
 Safety performance and incident learnings will be shared openly during reviews
 Safety will be treated as a core planning factor, not just a compliance requirement
