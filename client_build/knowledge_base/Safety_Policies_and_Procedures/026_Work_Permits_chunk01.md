## Work Permits

Introduction and Background of the Section
Work permits are formal authorizations issued before starting any potentially hazardous maintenance activity. They are an essential element of a structured safety management system, ensuring that risks are properly assessed, mitigated, and communicated before work begins.
In maintenance operations—especially those involving electrical systems, confined spaces, hot work, working at heights, or high-energy systems—permit-to-work systems protect workers, nearby personnel, and equipment by:
 Requiring formal job preparation and hazard identification
 Enforcing specific control measures and isolations
 Ensuring cross-functional communication (Maintenance, Operations, Safety)
 Providing documentation for traceability, audit, and compliance
Work permits must be used in conjunction with other safety protocols including Task Hazard Analysis (THA), LOTO procedures, PPE usage, and supervisory sign-off. Only authorized personnel may issue, approve, or execute work under a permit.
This system is aligned with best practices from:
 ISO 45001 – Occupational Health & Safety Management
 SMRP Maintenance Safety Best Practices
 OSHA 1910.147 (Control of Hazardous Energy)
 NFPA 51B (Hot Work Permit Systems)
Types of Work Permits Used in Maintenance

| Permit Type | Purpose / Application |
| --- | --- |
| General Work Permit | Required for routine preventive or corrective maintenance tasks that do not involve elevated risks (e.g., inspections, lubrication, adjustments). Ensures task is tracked, authorized, and supervised even if no special hazards exist. |
| Hot Work Permit | Required for any operation involving welding, grinding, cutting, or open flames in areas with fire risk. |
| Electrical Work Permit | Required before working on or near live electrical panels, energized circuits, or installations. |
| Confined Space Entry Permit | Required for any entry into tanks, silos, pits, or enclosed areas with limited access or ventilation. |
| Working at Height Permit | Required for tasks carried out at elevations above 1.8 meters (6 feet) using ladders, scaffolding, or platforms. |
| Isolation / LOTO Permit | Required when isolating equipment from energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, and hydraulic) to prevent unexpected energization. |
| Excavation Permit | Required before any digging or trenching activities (primarily for facility maintenance or external works). |
| Chemical Handling Permit | Required for jobs involving direct contact with hazardous chemicals, including lubricants, solvents, or cleaning agents. |

Procedure for Work Permit Issuance and Closure

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Determine Permit Requirement**<br>• Based on the job type, location, and risk level (per SOP, THA, or historical experience), confirm whether a permit is required. | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 2 | **Request Permit Form**<br>• Technician or supervisor obtains the correct type of permit form from the permit issuing authority or SAP system. | Technician / Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 3 | **Complete Job and Hazard Details**<br>• Enter details such as task scope, location, time, equipment ID, associated risks, required PPE, isolation methods, and THA ref. | Technician | Per Task |
| 4 | **Verify Job Site and Controls**<br>• Conduct a site inspection to confirm all controls (LOTO, barricades, ventilation, etc.) are in place before approving the permit. | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 5 | **Involve Safety Officer (if required)**<br>• For high-risk tasks (confined space, hot work, first-time tasks), Safety Officer must verify controls and sign the permit. | Safety Officer | As Needed |
| 6 | **Authorize Permit**<br>• Designated authority signs the permit and authorizes the start of the job. | Permit Authorizing Officer | Per Task |
| 7 | **Display Permit at Job Site**<br>• Permit must be posted visibly at the work location until task completion. | Technician | Per Task |
| 8 | **Execute the Task**<br>• Perform the maintenance job under conditions defined in the permit. Monitor safety compliance and intervene if unsafe acts occur. | Technician | Per Task |
| 9 | **Job Completion and Site Clearance**<br>• After job is completed, clean the site, remove isolations, tools, and materials. | Technician | Per Task |
| 10 | **Close the Permit**<br>• Supervisor verifies job completion and safe condition of equipment and area, signs off closure. Permit is archived (physically or in SAP). | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Task |
| 11 | **Record and Review**<br>• Maintain a record of all issued permits for traceability, audit, and learning. Include any deviations or incidents in the permit register. | Safety Officer | Monthly |

Additional Guidelines
 Permit Validity: A permit is valid only for the specific task, date, time, and crew listed. Any change requires reauthorization.
 Permit Expiry / Cancellation: If the job is not started within the validity period or conditions change (weather, equipment status, etc.), the permit must be voided.
 Emergency Work: In emergencies, work may begin under verbal authorization with immediate permit formalization to follow within 24 hours.
Permit Authorizing Officer

| Permit Type | Typical Permit Authorizing Officer |
| --- | --- |
| General Work Permit | Maintenance Supervisor or Line Manager |
| Hot Work Permit | Safety Officer (with Maintenance Supervisor co-authorization) |
| Electrical Work Permit | Authorized Electrical Engineer or Electrical Supervisor |
| Confined Space Entry Permit | Safety Officer + Area Production In-Charge |
| Working at Height Permit | Maintenance Supervisor (with Safety Officer consultation) |
| Isolation / LOTO Permit | Maintenance Supervisor + Electrical/Mechanical Authorized Isolator |
| Chemical Handling Permit | Safety Officer or Chemical Process In-Charge |
| Excavation Permit | Safety Officer |
