## Personnel Skill Development

Introduction and Background of the Section
Personnel skill development focuses on systematically identifying, upgrading, and tracking the knowledge and capabilities required to perform maintenance activities effectively and safely. It ensures that each technician, supervisor, planner, and engineer has the necessary technical, procedural, system, and safety competencies for their role.
Given the diverse and evolving responsibilities of the Maintenance Department—as detailed in previous sections (preventive maintenance, corrective work, spare parts management, SAP PM usage, calibration, safety, contractor handling, etc.)—the development of individual and team capability is both a strategic and operational priority.
Procedure

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Develop Maintenance Competency Matrix**<br>• Identify skill requirements for each role across technical, safety, system, and procedural domains. | Maintenance Manager / HR | Annually |
| 2 | **Conduct Skills Gap Assessment**<br>• Assess current skills of all personnel against the competency matrix using observation, testing, and supervisor input. | Maintenance Supervisor / HR | Annually |
| 3 | **Prepare Individual Development Plans (IDPs)**<br>• Design training plans to close identified gaps, including on-the-job training, classroom, and external courses. | Maintenance Manager | Annually |
| 4 | **Deliver Training**<br>• Conduct sessions covering equipment maintenance, safety protocols, SAP usage, calibration, and troubleshooting. | Training Coordinator / OEM / Safety Officer | Per Plan |
| 5 | **Evaluate Training Effectiveness**<br>• Use post-training assessments, job observations, and feedback to verify improvement in competence. | Maintenance Supervisor | Per Training |
| 6 | **Maintain Training Records**<br>• Document all training sessions, attendance, results, and certifications in employee files and digital system (e.g., SAP or HRMS). | HR | Ongoing |
| 7 | **Certify Technicians for Critical Tasks**<br>• Technicians must be certified before performing high-risk jobs (e.g., electrical isolation, confined space entry). | Maintenance Manager / Safety Officer | As Needed |
| 8 | **Review and Update the Competency Framework**<br>• Update competency definitions and required skill levels as equipment or practices evolve. | Maintenance Engineer / Manager | Annually / As Needed |
| 9 | **Encourage Cross-Skilling**<br>• Plan rotations or dual-skill training (e.g., mechanical + calibration) to build team flexibility and reduce dependency. | Maintenance Supervisor | As Needed |

Roles and Responsibilities – Personnel Skill Development

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | Actively participate in training programs, apply learned skills, and request support where needed |
| Maintenance Supervisor | Assess staff capability, recommend training needs, verify post-training improvements |
| Maintenance Manager | • Support technical content development, identify emerging training needs from equipment analysis<br>• Lead skill development strategy, approve IDPs, review progress, and authorize technician certification for critical tasks |
| Safety Officer | Conduct safety-related training and confirm technician eligibility for high-risk work |
| Training Coordinator / HR | Organize training logistics, maintain training records, and ensure alignment with HR policy |
| SAP PM Administrator | Record training qualifications in SAP where applicable (e.g., linking training to permit authorization roles) |
