## ii	Maintenance Software Training

Introduction and Background of the Section
Modern maintenance management relies heavily on digital systems to plan, execute, monitor, and continuously improve plant reliability and performance. At Arabian Mills, the SAP Plant Maintenance (SAP PM) module is the central platform used for managing all maintenance operations—including notifications, work orders, task lists, spare parts usage, calibration records, performance reports, and safety documentation.
To fully leverage the capabilities of SAP PM and ensure system integrity, structured and role-specific software training is essential. This ensures that:
 Data entered into SAP is timely, accurate, and complete
 Workflows for preventive and corrective maintenance are executed as per policy
 Spare part requisitions, inventory updates, and usage logs are aligned with actual activity
 Safety procedures such as THA records, incident reports, and permit linkages are properly documented
 Managers and engineers can extract meaningful reports and KPIs for decision-making
Importance of SAP Training for Maintenance Personnel

| Role | Key SAP Competencies Required |
| --- | --- |
| Technicians | Entering maintenance notifications, confirming work order completion, attaching THAs, and logging safety incidents |
| Supervisors | Approving notifications, assigning tasks, checking permit status, verifying feedback and closure |
| Planners | Creating and scheduling work orders, task lists, preventive plans, and MRP-related parts planning |
| Managers | Analyzing historical data, costs, downtime, and failure trends; updating strategies and master data |
| Directors | Monitoring KPIs, backlog, compliance reports, and validating budget reports |
| SAP PM Administrator | Maintaining asset master data, configuration support, training coordination, and user access control |

Training Guidelines
 All new maintenance employees shall receive mandatory SAP PM onboarding training appropriate to their job role
 Refresher sessions shall be conducted at least annually and also:
  o When SAP PM functionality changes
  o When gaps or errors in usage are identified through audits
 Training must combine system navigation, policy-based usage (e.g., THA and permit linking), and practical data entry exercises
 Technicians and supervisors must be evaluated on their ability to use SAP PM correctly as part of their individual development plans (IDPs)
 User manuals, quick reference guides, and job aids shall be made available at relevant workstations
