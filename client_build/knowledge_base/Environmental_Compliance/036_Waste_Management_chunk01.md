## Waste Management

Introduction and Background of the Section
Maintenance activities generate various forms of waste, including:
 Used oils, greases, and lubricants
 Spent filters, rags, and cleaning agents
 Scrap metal, broken parts, and replaced components
 Packaging waste from spare parts
 Contaminated PPE or containers
Improper handling of such waste can result in:
 Environmental violations
 Workplace contamination
 Unsafe storage areas
 Reputational damage
This section establishes structured procedures for waste identification, segregation, storage, and disposal within the maintenance department.
Procedure – Waste Management (Maintenance Department)

| S. No. | Procedure Description | Responsibility | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Identify and Categorize Maintenance Waste**<br>• Classify waste as hazardous (e.g., oil, chemicals) or non-hazardous (e.g., scrap). | EHS Officer | Annual / Job-specific |
| 2 | **Set Up Marked Waste Collection Bins**<br>• Use color-coded bins or drums for different waste types (oil, filters, metal, plastic). | Maintenance Storekeeper | One-time Setup / Ongoing |
| 3 | **Segregate Waste at Source**<br>• Ensure technicians dispose of waste immediately after job completion in correct bins. | Technician | After Every Job |
| 4 | **Store Waste in Designated Area**<br>• Move filled containers to central waste zone using sealed drums, trays, or trolleys. | Maintenance Storekeeper | Daily / As Needed |
| 5 | **Coordinate with EHS for Disposal**<br>• Notify EHS or authorized vendor for scheduled pickup of hazardous or recyclable waste. | Maintenance Supervisor | Weekly / As Scheduled |
| 6 | **Record Waste Generation**<br>• Log waste type, quantity, and date in a manual register or tracking sheet. | Maintenance Storekeeper | Daily / Weekly |
| 7 | **Train Maintenance Staff**<br>• Conduct awareness sessions on what waste goes where and why it matters. | Safety Officer | Quarterly |
| 8 | **Inspect Waste Handling Practices**<br>• Include waste bins and storage zones in weekly shop floor safety/environmental inspections. | EHS Officer | Weekly |
| 9 | **Include in Monthly Review**<br>• Summarize waste records and issues in the monthly maintenance and safety meetings. | Maintenance Director | Monthly |

Roles and Responsibilities – Waste Management

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Segregate waste at job site and dispose in designated bins.<br>• - Avoid leaks, spills, and mixing waste types. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Ensure bins are available at point of use.<br>• - Monitor segregation compliance. - Coordinate disposal with EHS. |
| Maintenance Storekeeper | • - Maintain bins and transfer containers.<br>• - Record quantities and type of waste generated.<br>• - Assist in vendor pickup. |
| EHS Officer | • - Identify waste types in job planning.<br>• - Ensure proper containers and labeling for hazardous waste<br>• - Provide guidance on waste categories and legal disposal.<br>• - Audit storage and disposal zones.<br>• - Approve vendor. |
| Safety Officer | • - Include waste management in technician training.<br>• - Report unsafe practices or environmental risks. |
| Maintenance Director | • - Review performance during monthly meetings.<br>• - Ensure corrective actions are taken for any violations. |

Maintenance Waste Bin Labelling Guide

| Bin Type | Label | Color Code | Waste Examples | Handling Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Used Oil Bin | USED OIL ONLY | Black with Yellow Text | Drained lubricants, gear oil, hydraulic oil | Use sealed drums with lid; store in shaded, ventilated area |
| Grease Cartridge Bin | USED GREASE CARTRIDGES | Dark Gray | Empty or expired grease cartridges | Do not mix with oil or chemicals |
| Used Filter Bin | OIL / AIR FILTERS | Blue | Spent engine filters, air filters | Line with plastic; seal used filters in bags |
| Contaminated Rags Bin | CONTAMINATED RAGS | Red | Oil/chemical-soaked rags, cleaning wipes | Consider hazardous; wear gloves when handling |
| Scrap Metal Bin | SCRAP METAL ONLY | Silver or Grey | Worn parts, bolts, broken tools | No oily/greasy materials; inspect before transfer |
| Plastic Waste Bin | PLASTIC PACKAGING | Green | Packaging wrap, containers, bottles | Flatten or compress to save space |
| Chemical Container Bin | EMPTY CHEMICAL CANS | Orange | Used solvent, lubricant, degreaser containers | Triple-rinse if possible; store under roof |
| E-Waste Bin (if applicable) | E-WASTE | White | Used sensors, control units, electronic cards | Do not mix with metal or plastic |
| General Waste Bin | GENERAL WASTE | Black | Unclassified maintenance waste, worn-out gloves | For non-hazardous, non-recyclable items only |

Implementation Guidelines
 Place bins near:
  o Workshop areas
  o Lubrication stations
  o Stores and dismantling points
 Post laminated signage above each bin.
 Train all maintenance staff on usage.
 Inspect bins weekly for contamination or overflow.
