# Environmental Compliance

Introduction and Background of the Section
Environmental compliance is a core responsibility of the Maintenance & Engineering Department at Arabian Mills, reflecting the company’s broader commitment to sustainable operations, regulatory adherence, and environmental stewardship.
While the Environmental, Health & Safety (EHS) function provides overall guidance and oversight, the maintenance and engineering teams are accountable for executing their work in a way that minimizes environmental impact and supports factory-wide sustainability goals.
The scope of environmental compliance within the department focuses on two key areas:
 Waste Management: Maintenance activities generate various forms of waste, including hazardous waste such as used oils, filters, and chemical containers, and non-hazardous waste such as scrap metals and packaging. The department is responsible for ensuring proper identification, segregation, storage, and disposal of all such waste in accordance with legal requirements and company procedures.
 Energy Efficiency: As the department responsible for maintaining all energy-consuming equipment and systems, Maintenance & Engineering plays a critical role in improving plant-wide energy performance. By ensuring optimal equipment condition, identifying energy-saving opportunities, and participating in energy audits, the department helps reduce the energy cost per ton of product, a key performance metric for Arabian Mills.
Environmental compliance is embedded into the department’s preventive maintenance tasks, calibration routines, equipment upgrades, and training programs. Through systematic waste management and energy efficiency initiatives, the Maintenance & Engineering Department ensures that its operations contribute to both environmental protection and cost-effective production.
Policy
The Maintenance & Engineering Department at Arabian Mills is responsible for supporting the company’s environmental objectives by integrating environmental compliance into all maintenance-related activities. This policy covers two core areas:
1. Waste Management
The department shall:
 Identify, segregate, and safely store all maintenance-generated waste (e.g., used oils, filters, scrap parts, chemical containers) in accordance with regulatory and company requirements.
 Use designated bins and waste areas for hazardous and non-hazardous waste as defined in internal procedures.
 Coordinate with the EHS team and approved disposal vendors to ensure environmentally safe and traceable disposal.
 Maintain accurate records of waste generation and disposal for audit and reporting purposes.
2. Energy Efficiency
The department shall:
 Maintain equipment to operate at optimal efficiency, minimizing avoidable energy consumption due to mechanical inefficiencies, air or steam leaks, or sensor inaccuracies.
 Monitor and support continuous improvement of the energy cost per ton of product, a key departmental KPI.
 Identify and implement maintenance actions and engineering upgrades that reduce energy usage across major systems.
 Participate in plant-wide energy audits and collaborate with utilities and operations to improve overall energy performance.
This policy applies to all maintenance and engineering personnel and must be reflected in planning, execution, documentation, and review of all relevant tasks.
