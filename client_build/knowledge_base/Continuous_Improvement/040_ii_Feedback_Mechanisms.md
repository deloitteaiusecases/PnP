## ii	Feedback Mechanisms

Introduction and Background of the Section
Feedback mechanisms are essential for sustaining a culture of continuous improvement. In maintenance operations, effective feedback ensures that valuable insights from technicians, engineers, and supervisors are captured, analyzed, and acted upon. These insights may relate to equipment conditions, procedural issues, safety concerns, improvement opportunities, or lessons learned from past work.
At Arabian Mills, formalizing maintenance-related feedback systems helps:
 Prevent recurrence of equipment failures or inefficiencies
 Improve the quality of maintenance planning and execution
 Enhance technician ownership and engagement
 Support accurate KPI analysis and RCA
This section defines the feedback mechanisms to be implemented within the maintenance department and how feedback is captured, reviewed, and integrated into planning and improvement actions. While enterprise systems like SAP can streamline data capture and reporting, effective feedback systems shall also be implemented using Google forms.
This section outlines how maintenance personnel at Arabian Mills can provide, track, and review feedback using accessible formats such as:
 Feedback logbooks or suggestion registers
 Printed feedback forms
 Excel-based tracking sheets
 Whiteboards or verbal reports captured during toolbox talks
The goal is to ensure that valuable field insights are not lost, even in the absence of automated systems.
Procedure – Feedback Mechanisms

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Define Acceptable Feedback Categories**<br>• Clarify and communicate what constitutes feedback: equipment suggestions, safety observations, procedural gaps, repeat issues, etc. | Maintenance Director | One-time / Annual Review |
| 2 | **Set Up Central Feedback Channel based on Google forms**<br>• Create a central f eedback l og on Google forms and provide access link to all maintenance staff . | Maintenance Manager | One-time Setup |
| 3 | **Capture Feedback via Google Forms or Meetings**<br>• Technicians and supervisors record their observations either on Google forms or during shift meetings and toolbox talks. | Technician / Supervisor | Ongoing |
| 4 | **Collect and Log Feedback Weekly**<br>• Supervisors submit all collected feedback to the Planner or Engineer for recording in the centralized tracker. | Maintenance Supervisor | Weekly |
| 5 | **Categorize and Prioritize**<br>• Classify feedback based on urgency and impact: safety-critical, improvement, procedural, training need, etc. | Maintenance Manager | Weekly |
| 6 | **Review in Monthly Maintenance Meetings**<br>• Include selected feedback in the agenda and assign corrective actions or follow-up as necessary. | Maintenance Director | Monthly |
| 7 | **Track Action Items**<br>• Update feedback tracker with action taken, owner, target date, and current status (open/in progress/closed). | Maintenance Manager | Weekly / Ongoing |
| 8 | **Close Feedback Loop**<br>• Update the feedback provider on actions taken to maintain motivation and trust in the system. | Maintenance Supervisor | Monthly / As Needed |

Roles and Responsibilities – Feedback Mechanisms

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Technician | • - Fill out printed feedback forms or voice concerns during meetings.<br>• - Suggest practical improvements from daily tasks. |
| Maintenance Supervisor | • - Collect technician feedback weekly.<br>• - Record verbal observations during shift meetings or toolbox talks. |
| Maintenance Manager | • - Log feedback in the central tracker.<br>• - Categorize feedback and coordinate follow-up actions. |
| Planner | • - Maintain the Feedback Tracker (e.g., Excel sheet).<br>• - Record status and ownership of feedback items. |
| Safety Officer | • - Collect safety-related suggestions.<br>• - Ensure timely review and closure of safety observations. |
| Maintenance Director | • - Chair feedback review in monthly meetings.<br>• - Approve corrective actions and ensure resolution of critical feedback. |
