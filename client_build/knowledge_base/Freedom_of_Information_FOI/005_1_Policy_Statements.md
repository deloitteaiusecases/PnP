## .1. Policy Statements

The below statements of policy are defined as the foundation of  view on freedom of information and should guide all actions in maintaining the standards of Freedom of Information across the . These statements are:
 shall create a Freedom of Information Plan to manage any requests submitted by individuals to access or obtain (unprotected) public information. The plan shall include, at minimum, the following:
  o Roadmap with the activities and key milestones for the implementation of Freedom of Information activities. The activities shall, at minimum, incorporate what is needed to achieve the specifications in this domain.
  o Assignment of the required resources and budget to manage and maintain ’s compliance with the National Data Management Office standards.
 shall launch awareness programs to promote and enhance the culture of transparency and raise awareness of the Freedom of Information policies and procedures approved by the Data Governance Council.
 The awareness programs should contain the important goals, objectives and implications of the NDMO standards.
 shall develop and follow the procedures to manage, process and document the requests to access Public Information.
 The process to access information should be transparent and easy to follow.
 The following four responses must be considered and analyzed for providing response to the request and the best suited shall be selected:
  o Providing the requestor with the information requested.
  o Deny the request of data with an appropriate reason.
  o Extend the time to respond to the request.
  o Inform the requestor if the information is publicly available or not under its jurisdiction.
 The needs to create the information request forms (both physical and electronic) which should ask the following questions:
  o Information about Data Requestor (name, address, email, national ID)
  o Description of the public information requested
  o Purpose of the request of information
  o Legal basis of the request
  o Notice delivery Method
  o Date of request
 shall set the necessary standards for determining the fees for processing requests to access or obtain public information based on the nature of the data, its size, the effort spent, and the time taken
 shall document all records of requests to access or obtain public information and the decisions taken on these requests, provided that these records are reviewed to address cases of misuse or non-response.
 shall be responsible for monitoring compliance periodically with Freedom of Information Interim Regulation and presenting the results to the Open Data and Information Access Officer. The corrective procedures should be determined in case of non-compliance and the Regulatory Authority and NDMO should be notified accordingly.
 A Freedom of Information register is required to be created which should contain the following:
  o Detailed records of the access of information
  o Information about Information Access Officer
  o Updated information about the current Open Data
  o Public Entity records and publications
  o Any other records required by the NDMO
