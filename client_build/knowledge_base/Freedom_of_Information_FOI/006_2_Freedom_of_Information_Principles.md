## .2. Freedom of Information Principles

 Transparency: Individuals have the right to access information related to public entities’ activities to enhance integrity, transparency, and accountability.
 Necessity and Proportionality: Any restrictions on requesting access to or obtainment of protected information received, produced, or managed by public entities shall be justified in a clear and explicit manner.
 Public Information Disclosure: Every individual shall be entitled to access or obtain (unprotected) public information; such Requestor shall not necessarily have a certain status or interest in this information to be able to obtain same and he shall not be subject to any legal accountability related to this right.
 Equality: All requests for data access shall be treated on an equal basis and in a non-discriminatory manner according to the NDMO’s Freedom of Information Regulations.
 Accountability and Reasonable Justification: Any restrictions on requesting access or obtaining protected information received, produced, or managed by  must be justified in a clear and explicit manner.
 Public Information Disclosure: Every individual has the right to access or obtain public information – unprotected – and the applicant does not necessarily have a certain status or interest in this information to be able to obtain it and is not subject to any legal accountability related to this right.
 Ethical Data Use: Data should be used with defined good governance practices along with transparency and traceability.
 Trusted Data: Storage and provisioning of access of trusted data to public should be a regular practice.
 Data Standards: Freedom of Information standards should be created, used, and maintained across  in order to support data integrity, consistency, and usability.
 Collective Accountability: All parties involved in the Freedom of Information activities should be held responsible for decisions, for processing as per the clearly defined reasons, and for taking the necessary actions to ensure data quality and implementation of FOI as defined in the Freedom of Information policy and as prescribed by National Data Management Office.
