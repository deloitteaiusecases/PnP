# Documentation

Introduction and Background of the Section
Effective documentation is a critical enabler of maintenance excellence, compliance, performance monitoring, and continuous improvement. In a facility like Arabian Mills—where preventive, corrective, and calibration activities are performed daily—structured and reliable documentation ensures:
 Traceability of maintenance actions and asset performance history
 Consistency in procedures, standards, and safety protocols
 Compliance with regulatory, quality, and environmental audits
 Decision-making based on verified, recorded data
This section outlines the standards and procedures for documentation and record-keeping within the Maintenance & Engineering Department, covering both paper-based and digital systems. While SAP PM is the primary digital tool used for maintenance documentation, supplementary records such as inspection checklists, calibration certificates, vendor evaluations, and safety logs must also be maintained systematically.
Policy
The Maintenance & Engineering Department shall maintain accurate, complete, and up-to-date records of all maintenance-related activities, events, procedures, and performance indicators. Specifically:
 All work orders, inspection checklists, calibration reports, RCA forms, permits, training records, and vendor documents must be recorded, reviewed, and archived according to internal documentation protocols.
 Records must be accessible for internal performance review, management reporting, and external audits.
 SAP PM shall serve as the central repository for all digitally supported maintenance transactions, while structured templates shall be used for manual records.
 All documents must be version-controlled, signed or electronically verified where required, and retained as per company-defined retention periods.
