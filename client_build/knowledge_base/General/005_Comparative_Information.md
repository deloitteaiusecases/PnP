## Comparative Information

As required by IFRS and to help users of financial statements to identify and assess changes and trends, the financial statements shall also provide comparative information for at least one preceding reporting period, except for the first financial year of the Company, where no such information is available.
Information about possible future transactions and other possible future events (forward-looking information) shall be included in the financial statements if it:
 Relates to the Company’s assets or liabilities (including unrecognized assets or liabilities) or equity that existed at the end of the reporting period, or during the reporting period, or to income or expenses for the reporting period; and
 Is useful to the users of financial statements.
