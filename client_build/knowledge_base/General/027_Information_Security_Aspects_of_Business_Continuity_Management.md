## Information Security Aspects of Business Continuity Management

1. Requirements: Define, document, approve, and implement cybersecurity requirements for business continuity management.
2. Continuity Determination: Determine requirements for information security and continuity of information security management in adverse situations, e.g., during a crisis or disaster.
3. Integration: Capture the continuity of information security within the business continuity management process or disaster recovery management process.
4. Processes and Controls: Establish, document, implement, and maintain processes, procedures, and controls to ensure the required level of continuity for information security during adverse situations.
5. Response and Recovery: Develop, approve, and implement documented plans, response, and recovery procedures.
6. Review Validity: Review the validity and effectiveness of information security continuity measures when systems, processes, procedures, controls, or management processes change.
7. Verification: Verify established and implemented information security continuity controls at regular intervals to ensure validity and effectiveness during adverse situations.
8. Redundancy: Implement redundancy in information processing facilities sufficient to meet availability requirements.
9. Failover Testing: Test redundant information systems to ensure failover from one component to another works as intended.
10. Regular Testing: Perform regular testing for information security business continuity and document lessons learned for future reference.
11. Periodic Review: Review cybersecurity requirements for business continuity management periodically.
