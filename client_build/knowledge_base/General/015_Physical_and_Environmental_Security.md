## Physical and Environmental Security

1. Security Perimeters: Define and implement security perimeters to protect areas containing sensitive or critical information and information processing facilities.
2. Secure Areas: Protect secure areas (datacentre, disaster recovery centre, sensitive information processing facilities, security surveillance centre, network cabinets) with appropriate entry controls to avoid unauthorized access.
3. Office Security: Design and implement physical security for offices, rooms, and facilities.
4. Disaster Protection: Design and implement physical protection against natural disasters, malicious attacks, or accidents.
5. Working Procedures: Design and implement procedures for working in secure areas.
6. Access Points: Control access points such as delivery and loading areas to avoid unauthorized access.
7. Environmental Protection: Site and protect equipment to reduce risks from environmental threats and hazards and opportunities for unauthorized access.
8. Power Protection: Protect equipment from power failures and other disruptions caused by failures in supporting utilities.
9. Cabling Protection: Protect power and telecommunications cabling carrying data or supporting information services from interception, interference, or damage.
10. Maintenance: Ensure equipment is correctly maintained to ensure its continued availability and integrity.
11. Off-Site Authorization: Ensure equipment, information, or software is not taken off-site without prior authorization.
12. Off-Site Security: Apply security to off-site assets considering the different risks of working outside Arabian Mills' premises.
13. Unattended Equipment: Ensure users provide appropriate protection for unattended equipment.
14. Clear Desk and Screen: Adopt a clear desk guideline for papers and removable storage media and a clear screen guideline for information processing facilities.
15. Periodic Review: Review the cybersecurity requirements for the physical protection of information and technology assets periodically.
