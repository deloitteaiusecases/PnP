## Network Security Management

1. Requirements: Define, document, approve, and implement cybersecurity requirements for network security management.
2. Network Management: Manage and control networks to protect information in systems and applications.
3. Service Agreements: Identify and include security mechanisms, service levels, and management requirements of all network services in network services agreements, whether provided in-house or outsourced.
4. Segregation: Segregate groups of information services, users, and information systems on networks.
5. Networking Equipment: Establish responsibilities and procedures for the management of networking equipment.
6. Public Networks: Establish special controls to safeguard the confidentiality and integrity of data passing over public networks or wireless networks and protect connected systems and applications.
7. Information Transfer: Implement formal transfer procedures and controls to protect the transfer of information through all types of communication facilities.
8. Transfer Protection: Design procedures to protect transferred information from interception, copying, modification, misrouting, and destruction.
9. Secure Agreements: Address secure transfer of business information between Arabian Mills and external parties in agreements.
10. Electronic Messaging: Protect information involved in electronic messaging appropriately.
11. Confidentiality Agreements: Identify, regularly review, and document requirements for confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements reflecting Arabian Mills' needs for information protection.
12. External Parties: Apply confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements to external parties and employees of Arabian Mills.
13. Compliance: Ensure confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements comply with applicable laws and regulations of Saudi Arabia.
14. Periodic Review: Review requirements for confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements periodically.
15. Segregation Controls: Implement controls for logical or physical segregation and segmentation of network segments using firewalls and defense-in-depth principles.
16. Environment Segregation: Consider network segregation between production, test, and development environments according to business requirements.
17. Wireless Protection: Implement a risk-based approach for wireless network protection using strong authentication and encryption techniques.
18. Network Services: Consider a risk-based approach for management and restrictions on network services, protocols, and ports as per business requirements.
19. APT Protection: Protect network and information processing facilities against advanced persistent threats (APT) using appropriate technology based on associated risks.
20. Periodic Review: Review cybersecurity requirements for network security management periodically.
