## Cryptography

1. Cryptographic Controls: Develop, approve, and implement cryptographic controls for the protection of information.
2. Risk Assessment: Identify the required level of protection based on a risk assessment, considering the type, strength, and quality of the encryption algorithm required.
3. Standard Development: Develop, approve, and implement cryptographic standards.
4. Legal Compliance: Ensure cryptographic standards comply with relevant laws and regulations.
5. Key Management: Develop, approve, and implement procedures for the use, protection, and lifecycle management of cryptographic keys, including generating, storing, archiving, retrieving, distributing, retiring, and destroying keys.
6. Key Protection: Protect cryptographic keys against modification and loss.
7. Equipment Security: Ensure equipment used to generate, store, and archive keys is physically protected.
8. Periodic Review: Review the cybersecurity requirements for cryptography periodically.
