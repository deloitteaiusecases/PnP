## Incident and Threat Management

1. Procedures: Define, document, approve, and implement processes and procedures for cybersecurity incidents and threat management.
2. Competent Staff: Assign competent staff for incident and threat intelligence management activities.
3. Event Assessment: Assess information security events to decide if they are to be classified as information security incidents.
4. Incident Response: Respond to information security incidents in accordance with documented procedures.
5. Reporting Procedures: Develop and implement procedures for reporting information security events/incidents.
6. Awareness: Ensure all employees and contractors are aware of their responsibility to report information security events/incidents.
7. Management Responsibilities: Establish management responsibilities and procedures for a quick, effective, and orderly response to information security incidents.
8. Incident Classification: Classify security incidents as per procedures for effective response and escalation.
9. Response Plans: Develop and implement incident response plans.
10. Escalation: Develop procedures to escalate appropriate incidents to the National Cyber Security Authority (NCA).
11. Threat Intelligence: Implement appropriate technology for threat intelligence gathering and handling, considering associated business risks.
12. Weakness Reporting: Require employees and contractors to note and report any observed or suspected information security weaknesses in systems or services.
13. Knowledge Use: Use knowledge gained from analysing and resolving information security incidents to reduce the likelihood or impact of future incidents.
14. Evidence Procedures: Define and apply procedures for identifying, collecting, acquiring, and preserving information that can serve as evidence.
15. Periodic Review: Review requirements for cybersecurity incidents and threat management periodically.
