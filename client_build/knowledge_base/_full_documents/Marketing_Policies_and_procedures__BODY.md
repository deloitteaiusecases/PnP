## Introduction
Arabian Mills for Food Products Company (the “Company” or “Arabian Mills”) is a Saudi Closed Joint Stock Company registered in Riyadh, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia under commercial registration numbered 1010465464 dated 10 Safar 1438H (corresponding to 10 November 2016). The Company’s licensed activities include Packing and grinding wheat, packing and grinding grits, semolina, and bulgur, manufacture of concentrated feed for animals, manufacture of livestock feed, wholesale of bakery products, trade of specialty and healthy foods, land transportation of goods, storage in ports and customs or free zones, and integrated office administrative services activities.
On 6 Jumada Al-Ula 1445H (corresponding to 20 November 2023), the shareholders of the Company resolved to change the name of the Company to Arabian Mills for Food Products Company
The Company sells products under following brands:

- Finah: Various types of products including Chapati Flour, Pizza Flour, Patent Flour, Superior Flour, Whole Wheat Flour, and Vitamin D – All Purpose Flour, Mainstream Pasta, Harees and Jareesh and Noodles

- Kamil: A range of animal feeds including Broiler Starter Feed, Pigeon Feed, Cobb Breeder Feeds, Layer Feeds, Livestock Feeds, Horse Feed, Rabbit Feed, and Experimental Animal Feed.

- Master Mills: Various types of products including Premium Flour, Pasta, Semolina, Cake Mixes, Gluten Free products, Durum Wheat Harees and Jareesh, and Oats.
The Marketing Policy and Procedure Manual provides clear guidelines for the marketing department operations of Arabian Mills. It ensures consistency and standardization of procedures by following industry best practices. The manual outlines policies, procedures, and responsibilities related to marketing activities and initiatives, promoting transparency and accountability. By adhering to this manual, Arabian Mills aims to implement clear role and responsibilities for enhanced operational efficiency.

## Purpose
The purpose of this Marketing Policy and Procedure Manual (the “Manual”) is to document the Company's marketing policies and procedures, ensuring implementation of industry best practices. The manual serves to achieve the following objectives:

- Standardisation of Process:
By establishing standardised practices and guidelines, the manual aims to promote consistency across the organisation.

- Clear Accountability:
Each section of the manual will precisely define the roles and responsibilities of all personnel engaged in various processes and procedures. This will ensure clear accountability, foster a culture of compliance, and minimise potential risks. Further, it promotes improved interdepartmental collaboration and communication to optimized operations.

- Decision Making:
The manual will serve as an indispensable reference for the Company's Management. It will assist in making informed decisions, ensuring adherence to industry best practices, and reinforcing a robust internal control environment.

- Achieving the Company’s Strategic Objectives:
This manual will ensure that all marketing activities of the Company are in line with the Company’s strategic objectives.

- Training and Development:
The manual will act as a comprehensive training tool for new employees, helping them understand the Company’s marketing policies and procedures. It will also serve as a reference guide for existing staff, ensuring they follow the correct procedures.

- Risk Management:
Identifies and mitigates business risks through well-defined procedures. Ensures that potential issues are addressed promptly and effectively, safeguarding the Company’s and brand perception.

- Continuous Improvement:
The manual will be a living document, subject to periodic reviews and updates to adapt to changing business needs, industry trends, and regulatory requirements. Continuous improvement will be emphasised to enhance the effectiveness of marketing operations.

## Marketing Planning & Budgeting
##### Policy Statement

- The Marketing Department shall consider annual marketing planning and budgeting as a key activity. To create effective plans, the department shall clearly understand the business strategic goals.

- The Marketing Department shall develop marketing plans based on business goals and in close collaboration with the Sales and Finance departments; plans shall not be developed in isolation.

- Before the start of the new fiscal year, the CEO shall submit draft strategic goals and plans to the Arabian Mills Board for review and approval by September of the current fiscal year. Once the Board approves the plan, the CEO shall submit it to the Marketing Department to prepare the annual marketing plan and budget.

## Advertising and Promotion
##### Policy Statement

- All advertising and promotional activities will be derived from approved Annual Marketing Plans and budgets.

- All advertising and promotional activities must align with the company’s brand guidelines, core values, and strategic marketing objectives.

- A formal campaign plan should be prepared for all major advertising or promotional activities, including objective, target audience, media channels, and estimated budget.

- All campaign plans should undergo internal review and approval by the Head of Marketing before execution.

- Budget estimates for each campaign should be reviewed and approved by the CFO and CEO prior to execution.

- Only approved creatives (ads, videos, POS materials) may be published and will adhere to regulatory standards and brand compliance.

- All content should be truthful, respectful, and free of misleading or unsubstantiated claims, especially regarding product benefits.

- Campaigns should be monitored throughout the campaign duration.

- Post-campaign analysis, including key performance indicators (KPIs), ROI, and lessons learned, documented, and submitted within 30 days of campaign closure.

- All advertising and promotion expenses should be tracked against approved budgets and reviewed for cost efficiency.

- Campaign documents (plans, approvals, creatives, reports) should be archived for a minimum of two years for audit and compliance purposes.

- External vendors and agencies involved in campaigns shall sign NDAs and follow company brand, confidentiality, and quality protocols. Refer SCM policies and procedures for vendor onboarding and purchase order issuance.

- All major or seasonal campaigns, either Brand building or Promotional activities should be communicated to all relevant departments 3 months in advance. Following types of promotional activities and campaigns will be usually undertaken at Arabian Mills:
  o Awareness Building activities.
  o Major Seasonal Promotional activities.
  o Consumer Promotions

- Any variations from approved annual plans, budgets, timings, or ad hoc promotional activities, including trade marketing, must take approval from CFO and CEO and be communicated to all relevant department via email.

- On 1st week of every quarter a Management committee Group meeting (MCM), comprising of all Departments Heads and CEO, shall be convened to Preview previous quarter activities and share upcoming quarter plans of all departments, especially Sales, Marketing and Supply Chain, for organization wide collaboration and alignment.

## New Product Development Process
##### Policy Statement

- Launching new products categories required significant resources, collaborated efforts and market research.

- All new product ideas should support the company’s strategic goals, market positioning, and brand promise.

- A business case must be developed for each proposed product, outlining objectives, target market, competitive positioning, and financial projections.

- A cross-functional NPD Committee shall be established with representation from Marketing, Sales, QA, Production, Supply Chain, Legal and Finance.)

- All NPD projects shall be managed under the supervision of this committee to ensure alignment and timely execution.

- Ideas may be sourced internally (staff, operations feedback) or externally (customer feedback, market trends, competitor benchmarking).

- All ideas shall be screened considering feasibility, profitability, scalability, and strategic fit.

- R&D shall lead formulation trials using lab-scale or pilot plant equipment.

- Finance shall review and approve costing, pricing strategy, and margin targets.

- Any new product shall have XX gross margin to be considered for launch.

- For launching any new product category, which is beyond core capabilities and expertise of Arabian Mills, third party manufacturer shall be used till significant volumes are achieved which can justify CAPEX expenditures.

- Marketing and QA shall jointly approve all packaging artwork and claims.

- A go-to-market plan shall be prepared, including product positioning, pricing, promotion strategy, and sales training.

- Moreover, while studying market, it will be equally important to understand consumer sentiments, therefore, Usage and attitudinal studies will be crucial to understand the category dynamics and consumer behaviour towards it. A 3 to 5 years innovation pipeline should be locked to work cohesively.

- Launch approval must be obtained from the CEO keeping in loop Head of Sales and as per the DOA.

- Sales performance, customer feedback, quality complaints, and cost metrics shall be monitored post-launch.

- A formal review shall be conducted 6 to 12 months after launch to determine success and decide on scaling, modification, or discontinuation.

- Lessons learned from each NPD project shall be documented to improve future projects.

- All internal Sample testing will be led by Quality team and R&D teams. At least two samples should be developed and tested against benchmark competitor product in blind testing.

## E-commerce Aggregators Customer Onboarding
##### Policy Statement

- All customers onboarding process shall involve detailed customer profile, category insight on customer platform such as customer insights, category size and competitors’ offerings etc.

- Only approved aggregator platforms with verified legal status and relevant reach in the target market shall be onboarded.

- Evaluation shall be based on platform reputation, user base, alignment with the company's brand, and commercial terms.

- A sales expectation along with annual joint Promotional Plan should be developed to have realistic expectation from the specific platform.

- All SKUs listed shall be pre-approved by the Head of Marketing.

- Any price variation or promotion must receive prior approval from the Head of Marketing and CFO.

- Only official, approved content may be shared with e-commerce partners.

- Updates (e.g., pack change, price change) shall be communicated within 3 working days.

- Marketing shall ensure the timely completion of platform onboarding forms, product sheets, and legal agreements.

- MRP and selling price on aggregator platforms shall match the approved Pricing strategy.

- Inventory allocation shall be coordinated with Sales and Warehouse teams to avoid stockouts or over-commitments.

- Marketing shall monitor pricing integrity across all platforms and address undercutting or unauthorized listings.

- All promotional campaigns (flash sales, bundles, discounts) shall be initiated by the Marketing Department.

- Post-campaign performance reviews shall be conducted to assess ROI and learnings.

- Queries, reviews, or complaints received via e-commerce partners shall be responded to within 24–48 hours.

- Monthly review meetings shall be done to keep track on the progress and any hurdles facing for business growth.

- A summary report shall be submitted to senior management quarterly.

- Action plans shall be developed for underperforming SKUs or platforms.

- Moreover, proper segregation of duties should be specified in the sale order process whereby sales order is generated by E com specialist after receiving Purchase order from customer, Supply chain shall be responsible for stock availability, verification and dispatches, and Finance for credit limits, data accuracy and invoicing.

- Marketing reserves the right to delist products or suspend aggregator partnerships due to breach of contract, pricing policy violations, or reputational risk.

- Any such action must be documented and approved by the Head of Marketing and Legal Advisor.

## Trade Marketing & Merchandising
##### Policy Statement

- Trade Marketing activities consist of store brandings, activations and events, Promotions plannings and Merchandising along with annual contracts negotiations and dealings with Modern trade and key accounts. This required greater collaboration and coordination between Sales, Marketing and Trade Marketing teams. To ensure smooth operations, a clear Role and Responsibility matrix shall be formulated and approved jointly by Head of Sales and Head of Marketing.

- A clear responsibilities matrix between Trade Marketing and Sales department shall be established and approved jointly by Head of Marketing and Head of Sales. :
i. Trade Marketing Responsibilities Should Include:

- Agreements including Joint Promotion Plans/Annual Promotional calendar and customer proposal negotiation.

- Activations, Promotions and Merchandising Planning including planograms and Executions.

- Brandings

- Stocks and other issues regular communication to sales to support timely information sharing and coordination.
ii. Sales Responsibilities Should Include:

- Agreement, Sales Targets, rebates, and discounts negotiation.

- Sales Order, timely supplies, logistics coordination and Returns management.

- Merchandising and other activities execution tracking as support to Marketing for close coordination.

- All trade marketing initiatives must align with the company’s overall brand and sales strategy.

- Annual trade marketing calendars must be developed in coordination with Sales, covering thematic promotions, seasonal events, and region-specific campaigns.

- Activities must be pre-approved by the Head of Marketing, and budgets authorized by Finance.

- Any promotional schemes (e.g., volume discounts, gifts with purchase, rebates) must be documented with clear mechanics, duration, and target audience.

- All schemes must be reviewed and approved by the Head of Marketing and CFO before implementation.

- Post-promotion analysis must be conducted to evaluate performance and ROI.

- All POS materials (banners, wobblers, danglers, shelf strips, standees) must be pre-approved by Marketing for branding consistency and compliance with advertising laws.

- Planograms must be developed for product placement by pack size, category, and SKU flow.

- Products must be front-faced, dust-free, and rotated based on FIFO (First In First Out) to minimize expired stock.

- Merchandising guidelines must be shared with distributors and third-party merchandisers with periodic training.

- Marketing-led in-store activation (e.g., live demos, tasting booths, mini-kiosks) must be planned with Sales for high-footfall stores or events.

- Activations must be documented with photos, attendance logs, and post-activation feedback.

- Trade marketing teams must collaborate with the Sales team to build retailer relationships and increase brand loyalty through engagement tools (e.g., co-branded signage, retailer rewards).

- Strategic partnerships with key accounts must be formalized through visibility contracts or listing agreements.

- Brand compliance audits must be conducted quarterly across key outlets.

- Customized promotions, special packs, or secondary displays for modern trade accounts must be approved by the Head of Marketing.

- Promotional activities must be synchronized with retailer calendars (e.g., Ramzan, Back-to-School, Health Weeks).

- Retail execution must be monitored using checklists and retailer feedback.

- Trade marketing team performance will be evaluated using metrics such as:
  o POS visibility compliance
  o Execution of promotions vs plan
  o Uplift in sales during campaign periods
  o Regular store audits and mystery shopper evaluations will be conducted to validate in-market execution.
