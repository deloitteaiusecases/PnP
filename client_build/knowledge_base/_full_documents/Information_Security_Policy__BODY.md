## Introduction
Information Security refers to the protection of information, which is considered critical for the survival of Arabian Mills. The security of data is built on three crucial pillars: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. Cybersecurity is viewed as a subset of Information Security focused on information assets in cyberspace. To ensure Arabian Mills' compliance with information security and cybersecurity standards.
The overall purpose of the Information Security Policy document is to provide guidance and directions for the protection of Arabian Mills Information Assets against threats to their integrity, confidentiality, availability, or deliberate damage. The Information Assets covered by these policies include all information, data, software, firmware, hardware, and communication networks owned, operated, hired, or used by Arabian Mills Group.
The specific objectives of the Information Security Policy are:
1. To prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, manipulation, or destruction of Arabian Mills Information Assets.
2. To ensure that physical and logical security controls are properly implemented and managed.
3. To ensure that users of information assets are aware of their respective responsibilities regarding Information Security.
4. To achieve adequate control of IT resources in a cost effective and efficient manner.
5. The objective of this policy is to integrate the ISO27001:2022 standard and Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-2: 2024) Framework, identifying identical and non-identical control requirements for implementation within Arabian Mills.
The Information Security Policy applies to any person who accesses information using Arabian Mills Information Assets and include all staff, contractors, consultants, and agents engaged by or for Arabian Mills. Personnel must ensure that Arabian Mills information assets are protected according to its sensitivity, proprietary nature, and importance.
The policy shall be reviewed at least annually or earlier if required, to account for major business changes.
Control Integration

- ISO27001:2022: 4 Domains, 93 Controls and Control Objectives.

- Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-2: 2024): 5 Domains, 29 Sub-Domains, 114 Controls.

## Purpose
The overall purpose of the Information Security Policy is to provide guidance and direction for the protection of Arabian Group Information Assets against accidental or deliberate damage. The specific objectives of the Information Security Policy are:

- Establish and maintain a robust framework for information security policies.

- Safeguard the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information assets.

- Define clear guidelines and controls for various information security domains.

- Foster a culture of security awareness and responsibility among all personnel.

- Mitigate risks and protect against potential threats and vulnerabilities.

- Ensure compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.

- Implement and enforce access controls to safeguard information and resources.

- Develop and maintain comprehensive business continuity plans.

- Manage and monitor supplier relations to mitigate risks associated with external relationships.

- Safeguard sensitive information through effective data protection measures.

- Implement physical security measures to protect premises, assets, and personnel.

- Establish and maintain a robust data backup and recovery strategy.

- Integrate security into the software development lifecycle.

- Implement security hardening measures for information systems.

- Promote the secure adoption and use of cloud services.

- Develop and implement an incident response plan for timely detection and response to security incidents.

- Collect, analyse, and apply threat intelligence to enhance cybersecurity posture.

- Ensure regular reviews and updates to policies to align with emerging threats and best practices.
Note: For this document, Information Security and Cybersecurity terms shall be used interchangeably.

## Information Security Governance
1. Strategy and Guidelines: A Cybersecurity Strategy and Information Security Guidelines must be defined, documented, and approved by the CEO of Arabian Mills. The strategy goals must align with business strategy, vision, mission, and related laws & regulations.
2. Structure: The Information Security structure of Arabian Mills must be defined and approved.
3. Committee Formation: A cybersecurity/information security committee must be formed.
4. Documentation: Relevant information security policies, procedures, guidelines, and standards must be prepared and approved by the cybersecurity/information security committee.
5. Communication: The approved policies, procedures, guidelines, and standards must be communicated to relevant internal and external parties.
6. Roadmap: A cybersecurity roadmap must be designed and approved for three years.
7. Periodic Review: Cybersecurity strategy and information security guidelines must be reviewed and updated periodically or whenever deemed necessary due to changes in the threat landscape, technology, or business strategy.
8. Annual Review: Information Security Guidelines must be reviewed and approved annually.

1. Requirements: Define, document, approve, and implement cybersecurity requirements related to Industrial Controls Systems and Operational Technology (ICS/OT).
2. Network Diagram: Maintain and keep up to date the ICS production network diagram.
3. Segmentation: Implement strict physical and virtual segmentation when connecting industrial production networks to other networks within Arabian Mills (e.g., corporate network).
4. External Connections: Implement strict physical and virtual segmentation when connecting systems and industrial networks with external networks (e.g., Internet, wireless, remote access).
5. Monitoring: Develop and implement procedures for continuous monitoring and activation of cybersecurity event logs on industrial networks and their connections.
6. Isolation: Implement isolation of Safety Instrumental Systems (SIS) by the Arabian Mills Plant Manager.
7. External Media: Strictly limit the use of external storage media and connecting mobile devices to industrial production networks.
8. Configuration Review: Develop and implement a periodic review of the secure configuration and hardening of industrial, automated support systems, and devices.
9. Patch Management: Conduct regular vulnerability and patch management for ICS/OT by the Cybersecurity Manager/IT & Cybersecurity Manager.
10. APT Protection: Protect ICS/OT against advanced persistent threats (APT) and malware attacks.
11. Periodic Review: Review cybersecurity requirements related to ICS/OT periodically.
