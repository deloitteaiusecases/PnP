## Accounting principles and policies
## A.Accounting Concept

Arabian Mills financial reporting framework establishes various fundamental accounting concepts on which a set of financial statements shall be based. Following paragraphs provide more information on the accounting concepts:

- Fundamental characteristics of financial information: The financial information presented in the financial statements of the Company must be relevant and faithfully represent what it purports to represent.
o Relevance: The financial information presented by Arabian Mills is relevant for financial decision-making processes. For financial information to be relevant, it must possess:
   Confirmatory value: provides feedback about previous evaluations.
   Predictive value: used as an input to processes employed by users to predict future outcomes
o True and fair view: The financial statements must give a true and fair view of the state of affairs of Arabian Mills at the end of the financial year and / or financial reporting period as required and of the results of the year or period. To present a true and fair view, the financial statements must be complete, neutral, and free from error.

- Enhancing characteristics of financial information: The usefulness of financial information is enhanced if it is comparable, verifiable, timely and understandable.
o Comparability: Arabian Mills financial statements is comparable, with consistent accounting standards and policies applied throughout each accounting period, enabling users to draw insightful conclusions about the trends and performance of Arabian Mills over time.
o Verifiability: The characteristic of verifiability provides assurance that the information faithfully represents what it purports to be representing.
o Timeliness: The financial information of Arabian Mills must be presented within a timescale that is suitable to the users for their decision-making process.
o Understandability: The classification, characterization, and presentation of the financial information of Arabian Mills is such that it is understandable to those who might want to review and use it.

These financial statements must be prepared under the historical cost convention, using the accrual basis of accounting except for employees’ defined benefit liabilities which is recognised at the present value of future obligations using the projected unit credit method (IAS 19); the measurement of financial assets classified as fair value through other comprehensive income (FVOCI), financial assets that have been measured at fair value through profit or loss (FVTPL), finance lease obligations which are valued at the net present values of related obligations (IFRS 16), inventory which is stated at the lower of cost or net realizable value (IAS 2), provisions which are the liabilities of uncertain timings or amounts, are measured and recognized at the best estimate of expenditure required to settle the present obligation at the end of the reporting period (IAS 37), and assets and liabilities acquired in business combination are valued at fair value with some exceptions (such as deferred tax, pension obligations, assets held for sale, etc.) as a method of allocating the historical cost of acquisition as a whole to individual items acquired.

The Company’s financial statements shall be prepared for a specified period and provide information about:

- Assets and liabilities (including unrecognized assets and liabilities) and equity that existed at the end of the reporting period, or during the reporting period; and

- Income and expenses for the reporting period.

The financial year of Arabian Mills is based on the Gregorian calendar year (starts on 01st January and ends on 31st December).

As required by IFRS and to help users of financial statements to identify and assess changes and trends, the financial statements shall also provide comparative information for at least one preceding reporting period, except for the first financial year of the Company, where no such information is available.
Information about possible future transactions and other possible future events (forward-looking information) shall be included in the financial statements if it:

- Relates to the Company’s assets or liabilities (including unrecognized assets or liabilities) or equity that existed at the end of the reporting period, or during the reporting period, or to income or expenses for the reporting period; and

- Is useful to the users of financial statements.

The financial statements must be prepared assuming that Arabian Mills will continue to operate for at least 12 months and the management has no intention or need to liquidate or curtail materially the scale of its operations.

Arabian Mills shall prepare its financial statements on a going concern basis unless management either intends to liquidate the Company or to cease trading or has no realistic alternative but to do so. When Arabian Mills (as the case may be) does not prepare financial statements on a going concern basis, it shall disclose the fact, together with the basis on which it prepared the financial statements and the reason why the Company is not regarded as a going concern .
When the Company is in the process of being liquidated or will be liquidated imminently, the financial statements might be prepared on a ‘liquidation basis.’ Under the liquidation basis of accounting, the reporting Company shall state its assets at their estimated net realizable values (fair value less cost to sell), liabilities and estimated liabilities (provisions) at their estimated settlement amounts. Value of assets shall not be increased where their fair value is greater than their carrying amount. Estimates will be periodically reviewed and adjusted. Claims, liabilities, and future expenses for operations will continue to be incurred with execution of the liquidation plan. These costs will reduce the amount of net assets available for ultimate distribution to the shareholders.

Gross accounting refers to separate recording of inflows and outflows in financial statements. Net accounting refers to netting off inflows and outflows in an agency relationship, so that the Company only recognizes impact to the extent that it is acting as principal.
At Arabian Mills, all assets, liabilities, revenues and expenses are recorded at their gross amounts unless Arabian Mills is acting as an agent of another company.

Offsetting is not allowed when it detracts from the ability of users to understand the transactions, events and conditions that have occurred and to assess the Company’s future cash flows. That is why assets and liabilities, and income and expenses, may not be offset unless required or permitted by an IFRS. On the other hand, measuring assets net of valuation allowances, e.g., obsolescence allowances on inventories and expected credit losses on receivables is not considered as offsetting.
The Company can present net gains and losses on disposal of non-current assets, including investments and operating assets, by deducting from the amount of consideration on disposal the carrying amount of assets and related selling expenses. Similarly, it can net-off expenditures related to a provision that is recognized in accordance with IAS 37 with the reimbursement under a contractual arrangement with a third party (e.g., a supplier’s warranty agreement).
Financial assets and financial liabilities are offset, and the net amount is reported in the statement of financial position if there is a currently enforceable legal right to offset the recognized amounts and there is an intention to settle on a net basis, to realize the assets and settle the liabilities simultaneously.
In addition, the Company can present on a net basis gains and losses arising from a group of similar transactions, for example, foreign exchange gains and losses or gains and losses arising on financial instruments held for trading. However, such gains and losses should be presented separately if they are material.
At Arabian Mills, financial assets and financial liabilities are off-set and the net amount is reported in the statement of financial position if there is a currently enforceable legal right to offset the recognised amounts and there is an intention to settle on a net basis, to realise the assets and settle the liabilities simultaneously.

Transactions and events are reported when they occur (accrual basis) and not when cash or its equivalent is received or paid. Arabian Mills prepare its financial statements, except for cash flow information, using the accrual basis of accounting.

Principles for recognition of items in statement of profit or loss and statement of financial position are as follows:

- Income is increases in assets, or decreases in liabilities, that result in increases in equity, other than those relating to contributions from holders of equity claims. Items that meet the definition of income are recognized in the statement of profit or loss.

- Expenses are decreases in assets, or increases in liabilities, that result in decreases in equity, other than those relating to distributions to holders of equity claims. Items that meet the definition of expenses are recognized in the statement of profit or loss.

- An Asset is a present economic resource controlled by the entity as a result of past events. An economic resource is a right that has the potential to produce economic benefits. Items that meet the definition of an asset are recognized in the statement of financial position.

- A Liability is a present obligation of the entity to transfer an economic resource as a result of past events. For a liability to exist, three criteria must all be satisfied: the entity has an obligation, the obligation is to transfer an economic resource, and the obligation is a present obligation that exists as a result of past events. Items that meet the definition of a liability are recognized in the statement of financial position.
At Arabian Mills, an asset or liability is recognised only if recognition of that asset or liability and of any resulting income, expenses or changes in equity provides users of financial statements with information that is useful, i.e., with:
(a) relevant information about the asset or liability and about any resulting income, expenses or changes in equity; and
(b) a faithful representation of the asset or liability and of any resulting income, expenses or changes in equity.

The trade date is the date that Company commits itself to acquire or dispose of an asset. Trade date accounting refers to:

- the recognition of an asset to be received and the liability to pay for it on the trade date; and

- derecognition of an asset that is sold, recognition of any gain or loss on disposal and the recognition of a receivable from the buyer for payment on the trade date.
The settlement date is the date that an asset is delivered to or by a Company. Settlement date accounting refers to:

- the recognition of an asset on the day it is received by the Company; and

- the derecognition of an asset and recognition of any gain or loss on disposal on the day that it is delivered by the Company.
When settlement date accounting is applied, the Company accounts for any change in fair value between the trade date and the settlement date in the same way as it accounts for the acquired asset.
At Arabian Mills, Purchases or sales of financial assets that require delivery of assets within a time frame established by regulation or convention in the marketplace (regular way trades) are recognised on the trade date, i.e., the date that the Company commits to purchase or sell the asset.

The Company prepares financial statements in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (“IFRS”), that are endorsed in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (“KSA”), and other standards and pronouncements endorsed by the Saudi Organization for Chartered and Professional Accountants (“SOCPA”) (collectively referred to as “IFRS as endorsed in KSA”). These financial statements must be presented in the functional and presentation currency of the Company which is Saudi Riyals (“SAR”) and rounded to the nearest Saudi Riyals (except when otherwise indicated).
