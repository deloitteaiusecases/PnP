## . Open Data
The Open Data Policy has been developed for  Saudi Fransi in compliance with the standards and regulations issued by the National Data Management Office (NDMO) of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Open data programs and policies are usually prepared and developed to support growth of the national economy and innovation agenda.
Open data programs and policies also represent a proactive step taken by  to uphold the right to access public information by making available and publishing a specific dataset of public information, as open data, ahead of being requested.

## .1. Policy Statements
The below statements of policy are defined as the foundation of s view on open data and should be a guide on all actions in maintaining the standards of Open Data across . These statements are:

- shall create an Open Data Plan to identify and coordinate the publishing of its Open Datasets.

- shall launch awareness programs to make sure that the potential users are aware of the existence, nature and quality of Open Data

- The awareness programs should contain proper usage of data. It should also contain various positive social and economic benefits.

- The process to Identify Open datasets should be done in-line with Data Classification policy.

- An appropriate open data identification process needs to be designed and followed.

- The open data identification process should include:
o Identification and documentation of all data categorized as ‘public data’ as per Data Classification
o A data valuation process should be created and followed in order to have a uniform decision-making process to decide whether the particular dataset should be published as open data or not
o Unauthorized disclosure of personal or sensitive information which could create any security risk should be analyzed in line with Data Classification Policy.

- Standardized formats need to be created and followed for all the Open Data documents.

- The Open Data should be maintained in an appropriate manner
o The changes in the Open Data should be documented and the metadata updated periodically
o To ensure that the Open Data sets comply with the regulatory requirements, constant review of the Open Data sets need to be conducted.
o Key Performance Indicators need to be established in order to measure the progress of the Open Data Plan and to gather statistics.
o For easy access and navigation of the Open Data, the necessary metadata of Open datasets should also be maintained.

- An Open Data register is required to be maintained to log all the activities related to the Open Data.

## .2. Open Data Principles
Open data is one of the NDMO domains for data management.  should adhere to the following principles:
1. Open by Default:  should be ensured that the data should be available for all through disclosing, providing access to, or using such data, unless its nature requires non-disclosure or protection of its privacy or confidentiality.
2. Open Format and Machine-Readable: Data shall be stored in different file formats (such as CSV, XLS, JSON, XML) and made publicly accessible in a machine-readable format that allows automated processing.
3. Up To Date:  shall be regularly published the latest versions of open datasets and made available to the public in a timely basis. Whenever feasible, data collected by  shall be released as quickly as it is gathered. Priority shall be given to data whose utility is time sensitive.
4. Comprehensive: Open datasets shall be as complete and as granular as possible, reflecting recorded data, in compliance with the Personal Data Privacy Policy. Metadata that defines and explains raw data shall be included along with explanations or formulas for how data was extracted or calculated.
5. Non-discriminatory: Open datasets shall be made available for all without discrimination or requirement for registration. Any person shall be able to access the published open data at any time without having to identify him or to provide justification for gaining access.
6. Free of Charge: Open data shall be made available for all free of charge.
7. Improved Governance and Citizen Engagement: Open data shall enable data access and engagement for all, reinforce the transparency and accountability of , and support the process of decision-making and provision of public services
8. Inclusive Development and Innovation:  shall play an active role in promoting the reuse of open data and providing the necessary supporting resources and expertise. These entities shall, in conjunction with the relevant stakeholders, seek to empower a future generation of open data innovators and engaging individuals, s, and the general public in unlocking the value of open data.
9. Open Data Platform: Any datasets in the Data Catalogue that can be made publicly available shall be listed on the  Open Data Platform. The Data Catalogue shall include datasets that can be made publicly available but have not yet been released.
10. Language: All published data descriptions shall be provided in plain language.
11. Quality of data: All published open datasets shall include an indication on the age of the data and an indication on the quality expected from the data.

## .3. Roles, Responsibilities and RACI Matrix
The following roles and responsibilities are applicable to this policy:

- Data Management and Governance Leadership Team: The executive body of  data management & governance is responsible for signing off on any changes, exemption, and exceptions to this policy.

- Data Governance Council: The strategic body of  data management & governance authorizes Open Data information exercises, defines priorities and critical data, and is responsible for approving sharing decisions.

- Data Governance officer: An experienced business domain representative responsible for managing all data management & governance initiatives and changes. The data governance officer overlooks and manages the Open Data requests and monitor the data sharing approaches.

- Stewardship Team: The stewardship team is responsible for attending training and awareness sessions, implement Open Data request process steps, document all Open data requests, respond to queries from Data Owner and Open Data Officer, prepare and share request forms, maintain Open Data register, raise any issue related to Open Data requests, identify and document metadata for open data sets and support on monitoring KPIs.

- Head of : Head of  responsible for managing all data management & governance initiatives and changes. The Head of  overlooks and manages the delivery of open data to the requestor and maintains plans, approve or reject publishing of open data datasets, maintain timelines, budgets and ensuring that progress is made.

- Chief Risk Officer: CRO is accountable for Authorizing Open Data Plan and roadmap, Update Open Data policy, Monitor KPIs and is responsible for Approve any exceptions or changes in Open Data Policy

- Data Owner: The Data Owner is responsible for providing domain-specific executive-level support in open data operations and exercises, communicating the reports of the exercises across the business domain, support implementation of Open Data process, manage and monitor activities of stewardship team, resolve Open Data related issues raised by stewardship team.

- Data Privacy Officer: The Data Privacy Officer is responsible for supporting Open Data activities. Their deep knowledge of the ’s data sharing capabilities allows them to validate the requests, thus ensuring that data is consistent are in line with the required specifications as per the request. Responsible for carrying activities like identify public datasets, publish and maintain open datasets, support on updating Open Data policy, getting feedback for published Open Data and maintaining Open Datasets which are already published.

- Data User: Any individual interacting with the data without having direct control over it. The data users are responsible for raising any issues that surface while interacting with the data.

- Data Specialist: Data Specialist is consulted for create an Open Data Plan including roadmap and assignment of required resources and budget, authorizing Open Data Plan and roadmap, identify and document metadata for Open Datasets.

| Open Data Publication | Number of identified Open Datasets that have been published | Total number of identified datasets that have been published annually. |
| Open Data Utilization | Number of updates performed on published Open Datasets. | Total number of updates done on already published open datasets. |
